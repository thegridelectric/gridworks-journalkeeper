# Alert Reference

This document describes each alert generated by `alert_generator.py`. The alert loop runs every **5 minutes** (`main_loop_seconds`) and looks at the last **2 hours** (`hours_back`) of data from JournalDB.

---

## 1. Critical Glitches

**What it checks:** Looks for "glitch" messages from SCADA systems and flags any that have a `Critical` type.

**Exact trigger:** A glitch message is found in JournalDB with `Type == "Critical"`. Each unique combination of house alias and glitch summary is alerted on once.

**Adjustable parameters:**
- `hours_back` (default: `2`): how far back to look for glitch messages.

**What makes it turn off:** Critical glitches are tracked individually. Each one expires automatically after `hours_back` hours, at which point the same glitch (same house + summary) can trigger a new alert.

---

## 2. No Data

**What it checks:** Whether data is still flowing in from each house.

**Exact trigger:** Either:
- No data at all was found for the house in the last `hours_back` hours, or
- The most recent data point is older than `max_time_no_data` seconds.

**Adjustable parameters:**
- `max_time_no_data` (default: `600` seconds / 10 minutes): maximum age of the most recent data point before alerting.
- `hours_back` (default: `2`): the lookback window for data.

**What makes it turn off:** Fresh data arrives (most recent data point is less than `max_time_no_data` seconds old).

---

## 3. Zone Below Setpoint

**What it checks:** Whether any critical zone's temperature has dropped significantly below its setpoint.

**Exact trigger:** The difference `setpoint - temperature` exceeds `max_setpoint_violation_f` degrees Fahrenheit, **and** the setpoint was not recently increased (if the setpoint was recently raised and the temperature was within tolerance of the previous setpoint).

**Adjustable parameters:**
- `max_setpoint_violation_f` (default: `2` °F): how far below the setpoint the temperature must be to trigger.

**What makes it turn off:** The zone temperature rises back to within `max_setpoint_violation_f` of its setpoint.

**Note:** Only critical zones (from `layout.lite` messages) are checked. Non-critical zones are skipped. The garage zone in Elm is explicitly skipped as a temporary fix.

---

## 4. Zone Freezing

**What it checks:** Whether any zone's temperature has dropped below a freezing-danger threshold.

**Exact trigger:** A zone's latest temperature reading is below `40 °F`.

**Adjustable parameters:**
- `freezing_threshold` (hardcoded at `40` °F inside the function): the temperature below which the alert fires.

**What makes it turn off:** The zone temperature rises back to 40 °F or above.

---

## 5. Distribution Pump

**What it checks:** Whether the distribution pump is running (flow detected) when there is an active heat call.

**Exact trigger:** All of the following must be true:
1. A zone heat call has been active for at least **5 minutes** and started at least **5 minutes ago**.
2. No significant distribution pump flow (`dist-flow`) is found around the time of the heat call (flow ≤ `min_dist_pump_gpm`).
3. This condition has been detected **3 consecutive check cycles** (i.e. ~15 minutes).

**Adjustable parameters:**
- `min_dist_pump_w` (default: `2` W): minimum power to consider the pump "on."
- `min_dist_pump_gpm` (default: `0.5` GPM): minimum flow to consider the pump "flowing."
- `whitewire_threshold_watts` (default: `20` W, with per-house overrides for `beech`: `100` W, `elm`: `0.9` W): threshold above which a zone's whitewire reading counts as a heat call.
- Heat call minimum duration: **5 minutes** (hardcoded).
- Heat call must have started at least **5 minutes ago** (hardcoded).

**What makes it turn off:** Distribution pump flow above `min_dist_pump_gpm` is detected around a heat call. The consecutive-failure counter resets to 0.

---

## 6. Store Pump

**What it checks:** Whether the store pump is running when relay 9 is closed (meaning the store pump should be active).

**Exact trigger:** All of the following must be true:
1. Relay 9 has been in the `RelayClosed` state for more than **10 minutes**.
2. No significant store pump flow (`store-flow`) is found since the relay closed (flow ≤ `min_store_pump_gpm`).

**Adjustable parameters:**
- `min_store_pump_w` (default: `5` W): minimum power to consider the pump "on."
- `min_store_pump_gpm` (default: `0.5` GPM): minimum flow to consider the pump "flowing."
- Relay closed wait time: **10 minutes** (hardcoded).

**What makes it turn off:** Either relay 9 opens (`RelayOpen`) or store pump flow above `min_store_pump_gpm` is detected since the relay closed.

---

## 7. Heat Pump Not Coming On

**What it checks:** Whether the heat pump is drawing power when it is expected to be running.

**Exact trigger:** All of the following must be true:
1. Relay 5 has been in the `Scada` state for more than **15 minutes**.
2. Relay 6 has been in the `RelayClosed` state for more than **15 minutes**.
3. Neither the HP outdoor unit (`hp-odu-pwr`) nor the indoor unit (`hp-idu-pwr`) has drawn ≥ `min_hp_kw` in the last **15 minutes**.

**Adjustable parameters:**
- `min_hp_kw` (default: `1` kW): minimum power draw to consider the HP "on."
- Relay state and power reading wait time: **15 minutes** (hardcoded).

**What makes it turn off:** Either relay 5 or relay 6 changes to a state where the HP should not be on, or HP power above `min_hp_kw` is detected within the last 15 minutes.

---

## 8. Not in ATN

**What it checks:** Whether the ATN (Atomic Transactive Node) is in control of relay 5.

**Exact trigger:** The current boss of relay 5 is `auto.lc.n.relay5` instead of `ltn.la.relay5`.

**Adjustable parameters:** None.

**What makes it turn off:** The relay 5 boss switches back to `ltn.la.relay5`.

---

## 9. HP On During On-Peak

**What it checks:** Whether the heat pump is running during on-peak electricity hours.

**Exact trigger:** HP power (ODU or IDU) ≥ `min_hp_kw` at a timestamp that falls during an on-peak hour on a weekday. A 2-minute grace period is applied at the start of the 7 AM and 4 PM hours.

**Adjustable parameters:**
- `min_hp_kw` (default: `1` kW): minimum power draw to consider the HP "on."
- `on_peak_hours` (default: `[7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 16, 17, 18, 19]`): hours considered on-peak.
- Grace period at hours 7 and 16: **2 minutes** (hardcoded).

**What makes it turn off:** No HP power readings ≥ `min_hp_kw` are found during on-peak hours in the current check cycle.

---

## 10. Rebooting

**What it checks:** Whether a SCADA system is rebooting repeatedly (detected via a burst of `layout.lite` messages).

**Exact trigger:** More than **5** `layout.lite` messages from the same house are found within the same 5-minute window.

**Adjustable parameters:**
- Reboot threshold: **5** layout messages per 5-minute window (hardcoded).
- `hours_back` (default: `2`): lookback window for layout messages.

**What makes it turn off:** No 5-minute window in the lookback period contains more than 5 `layout.lite` messages.

---

## Global Parameters

These apply across all alerts:

| Parameter | Default | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `main_loop_seconds` | `300` (5 min) | How often the alert loop runs |
| `hours_back` | `2` | Lookback window for data queries |
| `ignored_house_aliases` | `['moss', 'orange', 'spruce']` | Houses excluded from all checks |
| `timezone_str` | `America/New_York` | Timezone for all time calculations |
